The Process

Three Steps. One Standard.

1
ACT 1: INTELLIGENCE

Submit Your Trip

Your organization submits trip details through a guided form. Destination, dates, participants, activities, and logistics.

  • Destination and travel dates
  • Participant count and demographics
  • Planned activities and venues
  • Transportation and lodging details
  • Special requirements or concerns
Takes ~15 minutes
2
ACT 2: REVIEW

Analyst Reviews Everything

A trained safety analyst conducts a comprehensive 17-section review. The Risk Intelligence Engine scores every destination using 5 government data sources.

  • 17-section professional safety review
  • Monte Carlo risk scoring simulation
  • 5 government intelligence sources analyzed
  • Every venue, route, and provider evaluated
  • Recommendations written for each finding
3-5 business days
3
ACT 3: DOCUMENTATION

Receive Your Safety Binder

Audit-ready documentation delivered with SHA-256 hash-chain integrity. Every finding, every recommendation, every emergency contact, every risk score.

  • Complete findings and recommendations

  • Tamper-evident cryptographic verification
Delivered digitally

Intelligence Engine

We Analyze Data You Can't Get from a Google Search.

SafeTrekr's Risk Intelligence Engine pulls from 5 authoritative government data sources and runs Monte Carlo simulations to produce probability-weighted risk scores.

NOAA

National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration

Weather forecasts, severe storm alerts, historical climate patterns for travel dates and destination

USGS

United States Geological Survey

Seismic activity, earthquake risk, volcanic alerts, geological hazards for destination region

CDC

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

Health advisories, disease outbreaks, vaccination requirements, travel health notices

GDACS

Global Disaster Alerting Coordination System

Real-time disaster alerts, earthquake/flood/cyclone monitoring, humanitarian impact assessments

ReliefWeb

UN Office for Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs

Humanitarian situation reports, conflict updates, country-level safety assessments

RISK SCORING

Probability-Weighted Risk Scores

Our Risk Intelligence Engine runs Monte Carlo simulations across all data sources to produce probability-weighted risk scores -- not simple averages, but statistically modeled projections of what could affect your trip.

  • Thousands of simulations per trip
  • Weather, seismic, health, conflict data cross-referenced
  • Scores update until departure date
The Review

17 Sections. Nothing Missed.

Every trip receives the same comprehensive analyst review. Here is every section your safety analyst evaluates.

Trip Planning

1

Overview

Trip purpose, objectives, organizational context, and overall scope of the travel plan

2

Participants

Participant count, demographics, age ranges, special needs, medical considerations, and chaperone-to-participant ratios

3

Itinerary

Day-by-day schedule, activity timing, transit windows, rest periods, and contingency buffers

4

Transportation

Ground transportation providers, vehicle safety records, driver credentials, and route risk assessment

Destinations & Venues

5

Air Travel

Airlines, airports, layover risks, baggage policies, and flight timing relative to destination conditions

6

Lodging

Accommodation safety ratings, fire egress, proximity to medical facilities, neighborhood risk profile

7

Venues

Activity venues, crowd capacity, structural safety, historical incident records, and accessibility

Safety & Intelligence

8

Safety

Destination-level safety assessment: crime rates, political stability, natural hazard exposure, health risks

9

Background Checks

Verification requirements for chaperones, volunteers, and third-party providers in contact with participants

10

Intel Alerts

Active intelligence alerts from government sources: weather, seismic, health, conflict, and humanitarian data

Emergency Preparedness

11

Emergency Prep

Emergency action plans, evacuation routes, rally points, communication chains, and nearest medical facilities

12

Issues

Known risks, flagged concerns, unresolved issues, and analyst-recommended mitigations requiring action

Documentation & Compliance

13

Documents

Required forms, waivers, permissions, insurance certificates, and regulatory compliance documentation

14

Evidence

Tamper-evident evidence chain, SHA-256 hash verification, audit trail integrity, and record preservation

15

Checklists

Pre-departure checklists, day-of checklists, post-trip checklists, and completion verification

16

Packet Builder

Assembled trip packet: printed materials, digital distribution, chaperone copies, and administrative copies

17

Approval

Final review sign-off, organizational approval workflow, stakeholder acknowledgment, and release authorization
